Yes, I'm sorry, I thought they explained it, but it's been a while since I watched it.

There's a set of prepositions called two-way prepositions:
an, auf, hinter, in, neben, über, unter, vor, zwischen, entlang.

If the action moves from one place to another, they take the accusative. If there is no action, or the action takes place in one location (swimming in a swimming pool), they take the dative.

More on two-way prepositions here: http://german.about.com/library/weekly/aa052101a.htm

Much more on prepositions here: http://www.dartmouth.edu/~german/Grammatik/Prepositions/Prepositions.html
